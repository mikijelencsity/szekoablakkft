# Conversion Principles

## Core objective
Turn visitors into leads with minimal cognitive load.

## Primary conversion goal
One action per page:
- Request offer
- Call
- Contact form submission

Everything else supports this.

## CTA hierarchy
- One primary CTA per viewport
- Secondary CTA must be visually weaker
- Never compete multiple equal CTAs

## CTA copy rules
Avoid:
- "Submit"
- "Send"
- "Click here"

Prefer:
- "Request a free quote"
- "Get a price estimate"
- "Schedule a site visit"

## Trust building elements
Must include:
- real project photos
- real people (not stock if possible)
- process explanation
- proof of experience

## Psychological triggers
- Authority (experience, years, projects)
- Social proof (references, completed work)
- Clarity (simple offer structure)
- Risk reduction (free quote, no obligation)

## Form design rules
- minimal fields
- no unnecessary friction
- mobile-first input flow

## Offer clarity
Visitor must understand within 10 seconds:
- what is offered
- for whom
- what happens next

## Key principle
Confusion kills conversion.
Clarity sells.
