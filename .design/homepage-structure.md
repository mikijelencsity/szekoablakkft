# Homepage Structure

## Core principle
The homepage is a guided persuasion flow, not a menu of information.

Each section answers one question in the visitor’s mind.

---

## 1. HERO SECTION

### Purpose
Immediate clarity + emotional positioning + CTA.

### Content
- One strong headline (outcome-focused, not service list)
- Short supporting sentence
- Primary CTA button

### Example direction (not final copy):
"Better windows. Better home. No stress."

CTA:
- Request a free quote

---

## 2. TRUST STRIP

### Purpose
Instant credibility

### Content types:
- Years of experience
- Number of completed projects
- Service area
- Guarantees / reliability signals

---

## 3. SERVICES OVERVIEW

### Purpose
Explain what they actually do

### Structure
Each service is a card:
- Name
- Short emotional benefit
- Visual (photo)
- Minimal description

Services:
- Windows
- Shutters
- Renovation / construction
- Painting
- Flooring

---

## 4. HOW IT WORKS (PROCESS)

### Purpose
Reduce uncertainty

### Steps:
1. Contact / request
2. On-site inspection
3. Offer preparation
4. Execution
5. Completion

Must feel simple and predictable.

---

## 5. FEATURED PROJECTS (PROOF)

### Purpose
Visual trust building

### Content:
- Real project photos
- Before / after
- Short context per project

No stock images allowed here.

---

## 6. WHY US (DIFFERENTIATION)

### Purpose
Explain why this company

Focus on:
- reliability
- experience
- clean execution
- communication

Avoid generic claims.

---

## 7. STRONG CTA SECTION

### Purpose
Decision trigger

Must include:
- short reassurance text
- CTA button
- minimal friction message (free, no obligation)

---

## 8. FAQ

### Purpose
Remove final objections

Topics:
- pricing
- timeline
- process
- guarantees

---

## 9. FINAL CTA

### Purpose
Last conversion push

Simple, emotional, low friction.

---

## Flow principle
The page must feel like:
- understand → trust → see proof → feel safe → act

No section should exist without a psychological purpose.
